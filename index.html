Port Scans Explained

A port scanner attempts to establish networking sockets with a range of ports on a destination target. Common methodologies include:

  • TCP Connect Scan: The scanner performs a complete 3-way handshake (SYN, SYN-ACK, ACK). If the connection succeeds, the port is marked open.
  • SYN Stealth Scan: Sends a SYN packet and awaits a SYN-ACK. If received, it responds with a RST instead of ACK to avoid establishing a full connection, which keeps logs quiet on older systems.
  • Banner Grabbing: Establishing connection and reading incoming headers immediately sent by services to identify exact applications and operating systems.

Mitigating Network Scanning

Administrators must proactively protect systems against unauthorized port scanning mapping attempts:

  • Firewalls & Access Lists: Restrict public accessibility of services like SSH, databases, or administration tools to authorized IPs only.
  • Rate Limiting: Use firewall rules (e.g., iptables, nftables) or intrusion systems to detect rapid connection attempts and block offending IPs.
  • Port Knocking: Hide administration ports behind a sequence of connections to predefined closed ports. The port opens only when the correct knocking pattern is detected.
